About

I'm a robotics engineer focused on the problems that make autonomous systems actually work in the real world — sensor calibration, SLAM, localization, and navigation. My work sits at the intersection of algorithm design and field deployment, turning probabilistic models into robots that can reliably operate outdoors.

At SUTD, I've led the autonomy stack across multiple government-funded robots, from a SGD 2.2M outdoor decontamination platform to multi-robot surveillance systems. Every project has demanded solving real state-estimation failures on deformable terrain, in GPS-denied conditions, with production-grade reliability.

I write primarily in C++ and Python within ROS2, and I care deeply about clean architecture, rigorous testing, and software that can survive field deployment.
